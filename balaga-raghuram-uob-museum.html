University of Birmingham Museum - T-Rex and Natural History

The University of Birmingham Museum is home to one of the most impressive natural history collections in the UK, and the highlight for me was standing face to face with a real T-Rex dinosaur skull. The museum is managed by Clare Mullett, the Head of University Collections, who has spent over 25 years building and curating this incredible collection. Clare has created teaching modules, curated countless exhibitions, established hugely successful artist-in-residence schemes, and founded the University Collections Study Centre. Her passion for connecting people, places, and objects really shows in every corner of the museum.

The museum is also overseen by Jon Clatworthy, the Director of the Lapworth Museum of Geology. Jon has over 20 years of experience in geology and museum studies, and he led the transformational redevelopment of the Lapworth Museum between 2014 and 2016, turning it from an academic departmental museum into a publicly engaging, outward facing university museum. Under his leadership, the museum tripled its visitor numbers and greatly diversified its audiences. The collections are Designated by Arts Council England as collections of national and international significance, and the museum has been an Arts Council England National Portfolio Organisation since 2018.

Walking through the gallery with its fossils, minerals, and historical artifacts, I had the chance to interact with the team and learn about the fascinating history behind each specimen. The T-Rex skull on display is a cast of one of the most complete specimens ever found, with sharp teeth that remind you just how powerful these ancient creatures were. It was truly a privilege to experience this world-class collection in person and see the dedication of the people who preserve it.
