Role: Data Scientist in AI-Driven Oncology Risk Stratification

I worked as a Data Scientist on a collaborative analytics programme between NHS hospital trusts and private hospital networks across the UK. The project focused on building a machine learning model to predict deterioration risk in adult oncology patients after first-line treatment, helping multidisciplinary teams tailor follow-up intensity and intervene earlier for high-risk patients. The entire system is deployed as a live interactive application on Google Cloud Run.

Duties and Responsibilities

  • Integrated multi-source clinical data from electronic health records across NHS trusts and private hospitals
  • Engineered 87 features spanning tumour biology, treatment pathways, comorbidity burden, acute care utilisation, and longitudinal laboratory trends
  • Developed and benchmarked four model architectures: logistic regression, random forest, SVM (RBF), and gradient boosting
  • Performed nested cross-validation with Bayesian hyperparameter optimisation for tree-based models
  • Conducted survival analysis (Kaplan-Meier) and probability calibration (Brier score assessment)
  • Applied SHAP explainability methods to identify key clinical drivers of deterioration risk
  • Validated the model through a temporal train-test split (Years 1-3 training, Years 4-5 prospective validation) across 1,842 patients
  • Translated analytical outputs into a three-tier risk stratification tool (low, medium, high) for clinical use
  • Deployed the interactive model as a Docker container on Google Cloud Run with a Python 3.11 backend
  • Documented the methodology and results for clinical governance and ethical review compliance
