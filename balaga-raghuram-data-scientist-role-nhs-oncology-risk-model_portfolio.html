Data Scientist, AI-Driven Oncology Research (ML Development Stage)

Feb 2024 - Jul 2024 · NHS and Private Hospital Network, United Kingdom · Indirect Contract

  • Designed and built a clinical AI risk-stratification model across an NHS and private hospital network to identify cancer patients most at risk of deterioration following first-line treatment, across a cohort of 1,842 adult oncology patients
  • Engineered 87 clinical features from electronic health records (EHRs), chemotherapy logs, pathology reports, radiology metadata, and lab results; managed 8.4% missing data using MICE imputation and deterministic NHS record linkage
  • Benchmarked logistic regression, random forest, gradient boosting, and SVM using nested cross-validation and Bayesian hyperparameter optimisation with a prospective temporal train/validation split; gradient boosting selected with ROC-AUC 0.801, PR-AUC 0.609, C-index 0.791, and Brier Score 0.161
  • Applied SHAP explainability (XAI) to surface the top clinical risk drivers: prior A&E attendance, nutritional decline, comorbidity burden, dose reductions, and advanced disease stage
  • Validated findings using Kaplan-Meier survival analysis and decision curve analysis, demonstrating significant outcome separation across risk tiers (log-rank p < 0.001) over 36 months; delivered a Low/Medium/High risk stratification tool deployed across NHS and private hospital MDT pathways
  • Maintained full compliance with UK GDPR, the Caldicott Principles, and the NHS Data Security and Protection Toolkit

Skills: Gradient Boosting, SHAP, Survival Analysis, Feature Engineering, MICE Imputation, Bayesian Optimisation, Python, Scikit-learn, AWS S3 (data storage), GCP BigQuery (data pipeline), NHS Data Governance
